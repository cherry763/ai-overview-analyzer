    Go First, formerly known as GoAir, was an Indian low-cost airline based in Mumbai, Maharashtra, which ceased operations in May 2023 and filed for bankruptcy, with the National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT) ordering its liquidation in January 2025. 
    Here's a more detailed look at Go First:
    History and Ownership:
    • Go First was founded as GoAir and commenced operations in November 2005. 
    • It was a wholly-owned subsidiary of the Wadia Group. 
    • The airline's objective was to commoditize air travel, offering seats at a marginal premium to train fares across India. 
    • Go First rebranded as Go First in May 2021. 
    Operations and Fleet:
    • Go First operated a fleet of Airbus A320 and Airbus A320neo aircraft. 
    • It had a network of 37 destinations, including 27 domestic and 10 international routes. 
    • The airline maintained bases at Delhi, Kolkata, Bengaluru, Kannur, and Mumbai airports. 
    • It launched its first international operations on October 11, 2018, from Delhi to Phuket. 
    Financial Difficulties and Bankruptcy:
    • Go First faced financial difficulties and filed for bankruptcy in May 2023. 
    • The airline's operations stalled despite investments from the Wadia Group and an emergency credit line from the government. 
    • The NCLT ordered the liquidation of Go First in January 2025. 
    • The airline owed a total of 65.21 billion rupees ($781.14 million) to its creditors. 
    • Efforts to restart the airline were thwarted by ongoing legal challenges and a lack of funding. 
    • A sales campaign attracted two bidders, but both bids offered little in the way of upfront cash. 
    • The Pitti/Singh bid fell over after the Delhi High Court allowed the deregistration and export of Go First's aircraft. 
    • The Sky One bid was ultimately rejected.
