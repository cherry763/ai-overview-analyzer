    To recall an email in Outlook, go to the "Sent Items" folder, double-click the email to open it, then click "Actions" (or "More Actions" in the simplified ribbon) and select "Recall This Message". You can then choose to delete the unread copy or delete and replace it with a new message. 
    Detailed Steps:
    1. Open Outlook and go to Sent Items: Launch Outlook and navigate to the "Sent Items" folder where your sent emails are stored. 
    2. Select the message: Locate the specific email you want to recall and double-click it to open it in a separate window. 
    3. Access Actions: On the top menu ribbon, find the "Message" tab. Then, select "Actions" or "More Actions" (depending on your ribbon view). 
    4. Choose Recall This Message: From the dropdown menu, select "Recall This Message". 
    5. Select recall option: You will be presented with two options:
      • Delete: This option will delete unread copies of the message from the recipients' mailboxes. 
      • Delete and replace: This option will delete unread copies of the message and replace it with a new message, allowing you to edit the original email and resend it. 
    6. Confirm Recall: Click "OK" or "Recall" to initiate the recall process. 
    7. Monitor the recall status: You will receive a recall report in your inbox to indicate whether the recall was successful, pending, or failed. 
    Important Notes:
    • Recall only works for recipients within the same organization and using Microsoft 365 or Microsoft Exchange accounts. 
    • You need to have access to the email in your "Sent Items" folder to initiate the recall. 
    • The recall process is not instantaneous and may take some time to complete.

      Can you recall an Outlook email after it has been read?
      Can't generate an AI overview right now. Try again later.An AI Overview is not available for this search
      AI Overview
      No, you cannot recall an Outlook email after it has been read by the recipient; the recall feature only works if the recipient has not yet opened the email. 
      Here's a more detailed explanation:
      • Recall Functionality:
        Outlook's "Recall This Message" feature allows you to attempt to remove a sent email from the recipient's inbox, but only if it's still unread. 
      • Read Emails:
        If the recipient has opened the email, the recall feature will not delete it from their inbox, and they will receive a notification that you attempted to recall the message. 
      • Limitations:
        The recall feature has some limitations, including:
        • It only works if both you and the recipient are using Outlook and are on the same Exchange server. 
        • It may not work if the recipient has rules that move emails to different folders. 
        • The sooner you attempt to recall the email after sending it, the higher the chances of success. 
      • Alternative:
        If you need to correct a mistake in an email that has already been read, you may need to send a follow-up email explaining the error or correction.
