    To recall an email in Outlook, go to the "Sent Items" folder, select the message, and choose "Recall This Message" from the "Actions" menu. You can then choose to delete or replace the message for all recipients. This feature only works if the recipient hasn't yet read the email. 
    Detailed Steps:
    1. Open Outlook and navigate to the "Sent Items" folder. 
    2. Select the email you want to recall and open it. 
    3. In the top menu ribbon, go to the "Actions" or "More Actions" menu. 
    4. Choose "Recall This Message". 
    5. In the recall dialog box, you can choose to: 
      • Delete unread copies of the message: This will remove the email from the recipients' inbox if they haven't opened it yet. 
      • Delete unread copies and replace with a new message: This will remove the original email and replace it with a new one, allowing you to send a corrected version. 
    6. Confirm the recall action by clicking "OK" or "Recall". 
    7. If the recall is successful, you should receive a notification in your inbox.

      Can you recall an Outlook email after it has been read?
      Can't generate an AI overview right now. Try again later.An AI Overview is not available for this search
      AI Overview
      No, you cannot recall an Outlook email after it has been read by the recipient; the recall feature only works if the recipient has not yet opened the email. 
      Here's a more detailed explanation:
      • Recall Functionality:
        Outlook's "Recall This Message" feature allows you to attempt to remove a sent email from the recipient's inbox, but only if it's still unread. 
      • Read Emails:
        If the recipient has opened the email, the recall feature will not delete it from their inbox, and they will receive a notification that you attempted to recall the message. 
      • Limitations:
        The recall feature has some limitations, including:
        • It only works if both you and the recipient are using Outlook and are on the same Exchange server. 
        • It may not work if the recipient has rules that move emails to different folders. 
        • The sooner you attempt to recall the email after sending it, the higher the chances of success. 
      • Alternative:
        If you need to correct a mistake in an email that has already been read, you may need to send a follow-up email explaining the error or correction.
